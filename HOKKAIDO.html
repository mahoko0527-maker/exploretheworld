HOKKAIDO

About Hokkaido

Hokkaido is the northernmost of Japan's main islands, known for its stunning natural beauty, outdoor activities, and unique culture. It offers a variety of attractions throughout the year, from skiing in winter to flower fields in summer.

Cities

  • Sapporo: The capital city of Hokkaido, famous for its beer, ramen, and the annual Sapporo Snow Festival.
  • Hakodate: A historic port city known for its night view from Mount Hakodate and fresh seafood.
  • Niseko: A world-renowned ski resort offering excellent powder snow and vibrant nightlife.
  • Furano: Famous for its picturesque lavender fields and beautiful flower farms.
  • Daisetsuzan National Park: The largest national park in Japan, perfect for hiking and enjoying hot springs.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Winter (December to February) for skiing and snow festivals; Summer (June to August) for flower fields and hiking.
  • Transportation: Hokkaido is well-connected by trains and buses. Renting a car is also a popular option for exploring rural areas.
  • Language: While major tourist areas have English signage, learning a few basic Japanese phrases can be helpful.
  • Accommodation: Options range from luxury hotels to traditional ryokans and guesthouses.

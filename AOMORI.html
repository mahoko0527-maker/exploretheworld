AOMORI

About Aomori

Aomori is the northernmost prefecture of Honshu, Japan's main island. It is known for its beautiful landscapes, including mountains, forests, and coastlines. Aomori is also famous for its rich cultural heritage, including traditional festivals and local cuisine.

Top Attractions

  • Aomori City: The capital city of Aomori Prefecture, known for its vibrant arts scene and the annual Aomori Nebuta Matsuri.
  • Hirosaki: Famous for Hirosaki Castle and its beautiful cherry blossoms in spring.
  • Shirakami-Sanchi: A UNESCO World Heritage site, this mountainous area is known for its beech forests and hiking trails.
  • Oirase Gorge: A picturesque river gorge with stunning waterfalls and hiking paths.
  • Lake Towada: A beautiful caldera lake known for its clear waters and scenic views.

Local Cuisine

Aomori is renowned for its fresh seafood, dairy products, and agricultural produce. Must-try dishes include:

  • Sushi and Sashimi: Fresh catches from the surrounding seas.
  • Ramen: Especially miso ramen, which originated in Sapporo.
  • Seafood Donburi: Rice bowls topped with fresh seafood like crab, sea urchin, and salmon roe.
  • Jingisukan (Genghis Khan): A grilled mutton dish popular in Hokkaido.
  • Dairy Products: Including cheese, ice cream, and milk from local farms.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Winter (December to February) for skiing and snow festivals; Summer (June to August) for flower fields and hiking.
  • Transportation: Hokkaido is well-connected by trains and buses. Renting a car is also a popular option for exploring rural areas.
  • Language: While major tourist areas have English signage, learning a few basic Japanese phrases can be helpful.
  • Accommodation: Options range from luxury hotels to traditional ryokans and guesthouses.

Airports

Hokkaido is accessible by air, train, and ferry. Major airports include New Chitose Airport (near Sapporo) and Hakodate Airport. The Hokkaido Shinkansen connects the island to Honshu via the Seikan Tunnel.
